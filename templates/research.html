Relevant Research Areas

This project draws upon research in several key areas related to document summarization and Large Language Models:

  • Extractive and Abstractive Document Summarization: Research on different techniques for automatically generating concise summaries of text documents.
  • Large Language Models (LLMs) for Text Generation: Studies exploring the capabilities of large-scale language models like Llama in generating coherent and contextually relevant text summaries.
  • Document Preprocessing and Text Extraction: Research on methods for effectively extracting text from various document formats, including PDF, Word documents, and images (OCR).
  • Evaluation Metrics for Text Summarization: Studies on quantitative metrics like ROUGE and BERTScore, as well as qualitative human evaluation methods, for assessing the quality of generated summaries.
  • Applications of Graph-based Methods in NLP and Summarization: Research on using graph structures (like those potentially created with LangGraph) to represent and summarize document content.

While specific research papers are numerous and constantly evolving, these areas provide the foundational knowledge for this project.

In the next phase of testing, we plan to use metrics such as ROUGE scores, BERTScore, and human evaluation to assess the performance of our summarization system.
